# ICAAP & RAF Testing — Audit Explanation

## Audit Objective

The objective of this module is to assess whether material risks are properly identified, owned, reviewed, capitalised and monitored against the Risk Appetite Framework.

## Regulatory Context

ICAAP is the internal process through which a bank assesses whether it has sufficient capital to cover its material risks. From an Internal Audit perspective, the objective is not to rebuild the full ICAAP model, but to assess whether the governance, data, assumptions, risk inventory and escalation processes are working effectively.

The Risk Appetite Framework defines the level of risk the institution is willing to accept. Breaches of risk appetite limits should be detected, documented, escalated and remediated according to governance procedures.

## Tests Performed

1. Material risks without assigned internal capital.
2. Material risks without assigned risk owner.
3. Material risks not reviewed in the last 12 months.
4. Risk Appetite Framework breaches.
5. RAF breaches not escalated to committee.
6. Internal capital concentration by risk type.

## Potential Audit Finding

Material risks were identified without assigned capital, clear ownership or recent review. In addition, certain Risk Appetite Framework limits were breached without evidence of escalation. These issues may indicate weaknesses in ICAAP governance, risk ownership, monitoring or escalation processes.

## Audit Recommendation

Management should strengthen ICAAP governance by ensuring that all material risks have assigned owners, documented methodologies, periodic review dates and clearly justified internal capital allocation. Risk Appetite Framework breaches should be automatically monitored, documented and escalated to the appropriate governance body.

## Interview Explanation

I used SQL to test whether material risks were properly captured within the ICAAP framework and whether Risk Appetite Framework limits were monitored and escalated. The objective was not to replicate the bank’s internal capital model, but to demonstrate how Internal Audit can use data analytics to assess governance, ownership, review frequency and escalation discipline.
